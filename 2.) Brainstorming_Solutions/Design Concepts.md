## General Concept

I envision a desk-clamp yoke inspired by the Thrustmaster Boeing yoke, using a hinged pivot that produces genuine pitch motion rather than the simple push–pull mechanism found in the Honeycomb yoke. By mounting the yoke on a clamp-attached frame with a dedicated pitch axis and realistic leverage, the design aims to replicate the tactile feel and control forces of a real aircraft yoke. This approach improves immersion, provides smoother and more precise feedback for fine inputs, and allows for adjustable preload and damping so the response can be tuned to match different aircraft types.

## Mechanical Requirements

- Model cylindrical shafts and internal box cutouts inside the yoke body to route wires cleanly and fit electronic components.
- Provide dedicated cutouts/spaces for two buttons: one for the ATC push-to-talk switch and one for the AP DISC switch.
- Using rubber bands, the yoke column and head should return to the center after being pushed or pulled.
